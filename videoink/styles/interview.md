# interview style

For podcasts, panels, and talking-head videos where the speaker's own
voice matters as much as the argument. Preserves verbatim quotes.

## Rules

- Structure the article around the speaker's own answers, not a neutral
  narrator. The speaker's phrasing is a feature, not a bug to clean up.
- Keep **at least four direct quotes** from the transcript, rendered as
  Markdown blockquotes (`>`). Choose quotes that carry a concrete claim
  or a memorable turn of phrase, not filler.
- Cut filler and tics inside quotes with `[…]` only when strictly
  necessary; otherwise leave the speaker's words intact.
- Use H2 headings for thematic shifts (new topic, pivot, objection).
- Between quotes, write neutral one-to-two-sentence bridges that set
  context — never contradict or editorialise.
- If there are multiple speakers, tag quotes with their name on a line
  above the blockquote (e.g. `**Alice:**` followed by the block).
- Open with a brief scene-setter: who is speaking, what is the format,
  what is the central question.
- Strip pre-roll sponsor reads and outro calls-to-action.
- Plain Markdown only; no HTML, no frontmatter.
