# newsletter style

For the conversational, you-focused tone of a weekly newsletter issue.
Aimed at a busy reader scanning in an inbox preview.

## Rules

- Open with a strong one-sentence hook tied to the speaker's core claim
  or the most counter-intuitive point — no throat-clearing preamble.
- Write in second person ("you") and contractions ("don't", "it's").
- Keep paragraphs to 1-3 short sentences; the reader is skimming.
- Use **bold** for the one phrase per paragraph the reader should not
  miss. Sparingly — one or two bolds per paragraph max.
- Replace section headers with **lead-in bold phrases** at the start of
  each paragraph instead of formal H2s, except for the article title.
- End with a concrete takeaway or question for the reader, not a
  rhetorical wrap-up.
- Strip all podcast-intro, sponsor, and sign-off material.
- Plain Markdown only; no HTML, no frontmatter, no separators like `---`.
- Prefer concrete nouns and numbers from the transcript over abstract
  phrasing. If the speaker said "three weeks", say three weeks.
