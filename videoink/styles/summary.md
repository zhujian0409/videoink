# summary style

A tight recap: someone wants the gist of the video in under two minutes
of reading. Prose kept to a minimum; structure carries the weight.

## Rules

- Target length ~300-500 words. Cut ruthlessly; if a sentence doesn't
  add a new fact, delete it.
- Structure: one H1 title, a single 2-3 sentence lede paragraph, then
  H2 `Key points` with 3-7 bullets, then H2 `Details` with one paragraph
  per major claim. Optional H2 `Caveats` if the speaker flags limits.
- Each `Key points` bullet is one complete sentence — not a fragment —
  starting with a concrete noun or verb.
- No rhetorical questions, no "let's dive in", no "in this video".
- Preserve all numbers, names, and dates verbatim from the transcript.
- If the speaker makes a prediction, label it as such ("predicts that…")
  rather than stating it as fact.
- Omit jokes, tangents, and self-promotion unless they carry the thesis.
- Plain Markdown only; no HTML, no frontmatter, no tables of contents.
